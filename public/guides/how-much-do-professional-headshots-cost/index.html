How Much Do Professional Headshots Cost in 2026?

Guide · Updated Jul 2026

Most professional headshots cost $100–$700, with major-metro executive packages running $200–$1,500+. A basic LinkedIn-ready headshot typically runs $150–$300, while personal branding packages with multiple looks start around $500. AI headshots are a newer, much cheaper option starting around $1.

The Short Answer

Most professional headshots run $100–$700, depending on who's behind the camera and where you live. In major metros like New York, LA, or San Francisco, expect $200–$1,500+ for executive or personal-branding packages. Corporate group sessions and studio packages with multiple looks sit at the top of that range.

If you just want a clean, usable headshot for LinkedIn or a company directory, budget $150–$300. If you're building a personal brand and need a full wardrobe of images, plan for $500 and up. There's also a newer option that didn't exist a few years ago: AI-generated headshots starting around $1, which we'll get into honestly — including where they don't belong.

What Drives Professional Headshot Pricing

Headshot prices aren't random. Three factors do most of the work.

Photographer experience and location

A photographer with 15 years of corporate clients and a studio lease in Manhattan charges differently than someone shooting out of a home studio in a smaller city. Experience, reputation, and local cost of living all get baked into the day rate. This is the single biggest reason two "headshot sessions" can differ by $500 or more.

Session length and number of looks

A 15-minute session with one outfit costs less than a 90-minute session with three outfit changes and different backdrops. Studios often price in tiers based on how many "looks" (outfit/background/pose combos) you walk away with.

Retouching and delivery speed

Basic color correction is usually included. Heavier retouching — skin smoothing, background swaps, teeth whitening — often costs extra per image. Rush delivery (same-day or 24-hour turnaround) can add a surcharge on top of the base session fee.

Price Breakdown by Type

Solo or freelance photographer: $100–$300

Independent photographers shooting headshots as part of a broader portfolio (weddings, portraits, events) tend to price on the lower end. You'll typically get a short session, a handful of edited images, and digital delivery. Good for LinkedIn, a personal website, or a resume photo.

Studio session: $200–$500

A dedicated headshot studio — with professional lighting rigs, backdrops, and a faster in-and-out process — costs more but delivers more consistency. Steve Glass Photographer's pricing page, for example, lists studio headshot sessions in this range for individual clients as of the date checked (steveglassphotographer.com, checked 2026-07-17).

Corporate, executive, and personal branding: $400–$1,500+

This tier covers executive headshots for leadership bios, personal branding packages with multiple wardrobe changes, and corporate team sessions billed per employee. Capturely's pricing breakdown puts personal branding sessions with extended shot lists and licensing well into this range (capturely.com, checked 2026-07-17). The price jump here isn't just vanity — it usually includes usage rights for marketing materials, not just personal profile use.

Major-metro premium: NYC, LA, SF

Photographers in high cost-of-living cities routinely charge a premium over the national average for the same service. NLA Lorphotography's Connecticut-area pricing, compared against typical NYC studio rates, shows a clear gap even within driving distance of each other (nlalorphotography.com, checked 2026-07-17). If you're headshot-shopping in a major metro, expect the corporate/executive tier ($400–$1,500+) to be your realistic starting point, not the exception.

Tier price guide:

  • Solo/freelance: $100–$300 — 1 look, digital delivery, basic edits
  • Studio session: $200–$500 — Professional lighting, 1–2 looks, faster turnaround
  • Corporate/executive: $400–$1,500+ — Multiple looks, usage rights, brand-ready assets
  • Major-metro premium: $500–$1,500+ — Same services, higher city cost-of-living baked in

What AI headshots are not good for

AI-generated headshots are not a substitute for a real photo where one is legally required. Passports, government-issued ID, security badges, and similar documents need a verified, unedited photo taken in person. Don't use AI headshots for these — that's a hard no, not a "usually fine."

Where they do make sense: LinkedIn, company team pages, dating profiles, social media, resumes, and personal websites — anywhere a polished photo matters but a notarized one doesn't.

FAQ

What's a fair price for a 30-minute headshot session?
For an individual with a solo or small-studio photographer, $150–$350 is typical for a 30-minute session with basic retouching included.

Why do headshot prices vary so much by city?
Studio rent, photographer demand, and local cost of living all factor into pricing. A session in a major metro can cost double the same service in a smaller city, even from a similarly experienced photographer.

Is it worth paying more for a "personal branding" package?
If you need usage rights for marketing, multiple outfit looks, or a broader library of images for ongoing content, yes. If you need one photo for a LinkedIn profile, you're likely overpaying for that tier.

Are AI headshots a legitimate cheaper alternative?
Yes, for non-legal use cases — LinkedIn, social media, team pages, resumes. They're AI-generated, not photographer replacements, and they're not appropriate for passports or government ID. Within that boundary, they're a legitimate, disclosed, and dramatically cheaper option.
